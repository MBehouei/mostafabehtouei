About me

Dr. Mostafa Behtouei holds a Ph.D. in Experimental Physics from the University of Rome "La Sapienza." His thesis focused on designing and measuring High Gradient Accelerating Structures. During his research, he have contributed to the Compact Light XLS project and designed a high-power Ka-band klystron at INFN in Frascati. He is well-versed in various RF devices and electromagnetics simulation codes, such as HFSS, CST Particle Studio, Poisson/SuperFish, GPT, ECHO3D, and programming languages like Mathematica, Matlab, Python, C++, and Fortran. Dr. Behtouei's expertise lies in Mathematics, Physics, and Engineering, with a strong focus on electromagnetism and complex RF systems analysis and design. His research experience in accelerator physics includes wakefield, impedance, and collective effects analysis. Currently, he serves as a Research Scientist at INFN-LNF, focusing on the impedance model and collective effects for the FCC-ee project. His work has led to multiple publications in reputable scientific journals, and he has collaborated with renowned experts in the field.
